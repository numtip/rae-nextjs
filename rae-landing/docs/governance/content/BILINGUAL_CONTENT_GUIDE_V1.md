# Bilingual Content Guide v1

## Purpose
Define Thai-English content standards for institutional communication.
Ensure clarity, parity of meaning, and consistent tone.

## Scope
Applies to navigation, page content, dashboards, and notices.
Includes academic, administrative, and public-facing materials.

## Editorial Principles
- Accuracy before brevity.
- Formal but readable institutional tone.
- Avoid promotional exaggeration.
- Keep terminology consistent across languages.

## Language Ownership
- Thai is the primary reference for Thai-first pages.
- English must preserve intent, not literal word-by-word mapping.
- Resolve ambiguity with subject-matter owner review.

## Bilingual Structure Rules
- Keep heading hierarchy aligned between Thai and English versions.
- Keep call-to-action intent equivalent across languages.
- Avoid mixed-language sentences unless required by proper nouns.

## Terminology Governance
- Maintain approved vocabulary for units, programs, and offices.
- Use consistent transliteration for names and places.
- Do not introduce ad-hoc term variants by page.

## Thai Readability Rules
- Favor short clauses and clear sentence boundaries.
- Avoid dense legal-style stacking in body copy.
- Preserve line breaks that support natural Thai scanning.

## English Readability Rules
- Prefer direct active constructions.
- Avoid jargon unless required for technical correctness.
- Keep acronyms expanded on first mention.

## Numbers, Dates, and Units
- Use consistent date format per language context.
- Verify unit conversions and symbols before publishing.
- Keep numeric facts synchronized across both versions.

## Metadata and SEO Safety
- Titles/descriptions must match on-page intent.
- Language-specific metadata must not contradict body content.
- Avoid keyword stuffing in either language.

## Accessibility Rules
- Link text must describe destination meaningfully.
- Heading structure must remain semantic and sequential.
- Avoid all-caps blocks for long Thai or English passages.

## QA Checklist
- Is meaning preserved across Thai and English?
- Are terms consistent with approved institutional wording?
- Are facts and numbers synchronized?
- Is readability acceptable on mobile?

## Version Control
Owner: RAE Content Governance.
Review cadence: quarterly and at policy changes.
