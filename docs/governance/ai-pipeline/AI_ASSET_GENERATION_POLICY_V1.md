# AI Asset Generation Policy v1

## Purpose
Define safe and consistent use of AI-generated assets.
Protect institutional quality, accuracy, and compliance.

## Scope
Applies to text, image, media, and layout assets created with AI.
Covers ideation, drafts, and production candidates.

## Governance Principles
- Human accountability is mandatory.
- AI output is draft material until approved.
- Institutional facts must be verified by human review.
- Sensitive data must not be exposed to unauthorized tools.

## Allowed Use Cases
- Drafting neutral content structures.
- Creating non-sensitive visual concept references.
- Producing variant summaries for editorial review.
- Assisting with formatting and consistency checks.

## Restricted Use Cases
- Publishing unreviewed factual claims.
- Generating official policy statements without approver signoff.
- Using confidential data in public model prompts.
- Producing synthetic media that implies real events without disclosure.

## Prompt Hygiene Rules
- Remove personal or confidential identifiers.
- Use explicit scope and intended audience.
- Request source-grounded outputs for factual sections.
- Keep prompt and output logs for audit where required.

## Review Workflow
- Step 1: creator generates draft asset.
- Step 2: domain reviewer verifies factual and policy alignment.
- Step 3: language review for Thai/English clarity.
- Step 4: governance approval before publication.

## Quality Controls
- Factual validation against authoritative internal source.
- Tone validation against institutional style.
- Accessibility validation for published assets.
- Provenance note when required by policy.

## Model and Tool Constraints
- Use approved tools only.
- Record model/tool used for production assets.
- Re-evaluate outputs after model version changes.

## Incident Handling
- If erroneous AI content is published, retract quickly.
- Record root cause and corrective action.
- Update prompts or workflow controls to prevent recurrence.

## QA Checklist
- Is the asset reviewed by a human approver?
- Are all facts verified?
- Is sensitive data excluded from prompts?
- Is publication approval documented?

## Version Control
Owner: RAE AI and Content Governance.
Review cadence: quarterly or upon policy/regulatory updates.
