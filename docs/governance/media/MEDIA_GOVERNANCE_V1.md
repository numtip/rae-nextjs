# Media Governance v1

## Purpose
Set standards for image, video, icon, and illustration usage.
Ensure institutional consistency, accessibility, and legal safety.

## Scope
Applies to all media under RAE web properties and dashboards.
Covers Thai and English content equally.

## Media Principles
- Use media to inform, not decorate excessively.
- Maintain academic and institutional credibility.
- Favor clarity, context, and source transparency.
- Preserve consistent tone across departments.

## Image Standards
- Prefer editorially accurate campus and research imagery.
- Keep color grading natural and non-aggressive.
- Avoid over-saturated filters and high-contrast gimmicks.
- Ensure subject matter aligns with page purpose.

## Video Standards
- Default to captions when spoken content exists.
- Keep transitions minimal and information-first.
- Use stable framing for interviews and institutional messages.
- Avoid rapid cuts that reduce comprehension.

## Icon and Illustration Standards
- Use a single institutional icon style per interface.
- Prioritize recognizability over ornamentation.
- Match stroke/weight to surrounding typography scale.
- Avoid novelty icon sets in core governance pages.

## File and Delivery Rules
- Web images: use optimized formats suitable for target browsers.
- Provide descriptive alt text for meaningful images.
- Ensure responsive sizing for mobile and desktop.
- Preserve source files for governance traceability.

## Source and Rights Governance
- Record source, owner, and usage rights before publication.
- Do not publish assets with unclear licensing.
- Credit external sources where required by license.
- Remove or replace expired licensed assets promptly.

## Sensitive Content Rules
- Do not expose confidential student or staff information.
- Blur or omit identifiable personal data when required.
- Apply institutional approval for high-sensitivity media.

## Accessibility Rules
- Avoid text embedded in images when live text is possible.
- If text-in-image is required, maintain AA contrast equivalent.
- Ensure key visual meaning is not color-only.

## QA Checklist
- Is the media relevant to the page objective?
- Are rights and source records complete?
- Is accessibility coverage present (alt/captions/contrast)?
- Does the media match institutional tone?

## Version Control
Owner: RAE Content and Design Governance.
Review cadence: biannual and before major campaigns.
